How far can the SGA Thunder Go?

The Thunder's Game 1 win against the Pelicans showcased their ability to overcome playoff inexperience. Despite the majority of their players being in their first playoff game, they managed to secure a narrow victory. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander's leadership was evident as he made crucial plays down the stretch, including a game-winning three-point play. Additionally, rookies Chet Holmgren and Cason Wallace made significant defensive contributions, with Holmgren blocking five shots, including a pivotal one in the final moments of the game. The Thunder's victory was hard-fought, with both teams struggling offensively early on and the game remaining close throughout.

However, Oklahoma City's defense proved to be a deciding factor, holding the Pelicans to a low shooting percentage and effectively contesting their three-point attempts. Despite this promising start, Thunder coach Mark Daigneault emphasized that it's just the beginning of the series and highlighted the importance of continual improvement. As they move forward in the playoffs, the Thunder will need to build on their strengths and address any areas of weakness to maintain their momentum.
